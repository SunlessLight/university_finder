Tier 3 — The Apply Pack

The how-to-apply

Now go do it — nothing missed.
  • A ranked recommendation of your finalists + a realistic apply strategy (UCAS’s 5-choice cap, Oxbridge either/or, app-fee budget)
  • One deadline calendar across every country — applications, scholarships, tests, visa lead times, in order
  • A per-region apply guide (PDF) — US / UK / Australia / Singapore / China, grouped by system so shared work is done once

  • Real financial aid — what it covers and what you still pay (flights, visa, the student contribution “100% of need” hides), in USD + approx MYR
“Can’t I just use Gemini?”
Honestly, for a lot of it — yes. Three things it won’t do, and it’s not a prompting skill you can fix:  

1. It ranks unis you can’t get into as #1

It can’t tell “great uni” apart from “you can actually get in.” Mine keeps those in two separate columns — did your list tell you which ones were reaches?

2. It won’t say when it’s guessing

It’ll give you a 2022 tuition fee as current fact. Every number here is checked against the official page — and each row is stamped with whether it’s been verified yet.

3. It forgets you

Applications take six months. Next week it starts cold and gives a different answer. This is written into files you keep — grades, budget, dealbreakers and all.

When you don’t need me

If you already know your course, know your country, and have your 3–4 unis picked — use Gemini or the UCAS site. Genuinely.

Where it earns its keep: comparing across countries in ringgit · course still undecided · budget is the deciding factor · a regulated career (medicine / engineering / law) that must be recognised back home.
