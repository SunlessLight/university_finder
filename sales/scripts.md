# Scripts — copy, paste, adjust

WhatsApp-ready. Deliberately unpolished — if it reads like marketing copy, it reads like a scam.
The reasoning behind all of this is in [positioning.md](positioning.md).

**Two rules before you send anything:**

1. **Have the demo ready first.** A pitch with nothing to show is just claims, which is the exact
   thing they don't believe. See [§ The demo drop](#the-demo-drop).
2. **Never argue with Gemini.** Show yours, shut up, let the gap do the work.

---

## The demo drop

This is the workhorse. Not really a script — you just paste the columns and stop.

> this is what i track per uni btw
>
> ```
> List status · Desirability · Tier · Admission likelihood · Warnings · University · Course ·
> Country · City · Subject rank · Overall rank · Entry requirements · Student grades · Fits grades? ·
> English req · Backup entry route · Annual tuition · Total tuition · Est. living/yr ·
> Duration (yrs) · Approx total (MYR) · Scholarship & portal · Scholarship coverage ·
> Scholarship competitiveness · How to get the scholarship · Money to show (visa) ·
> Work rights after graduating · Recognised in Malaysia? · How to apply · Key deadline ·
> Intake · Notes · Course URL · Info source
> ```
>
> 34 columns, for every uni on the list

Then **nothing**. Do not add "see, Gemini can't do that." Let them arrive at it.

## 1. Opener — friends

> eh you doing uni applications this year right
>
> i built a thing that does the whole research bit — longlist of like 20-40 unis, cuts down to a
> shortlist, then an actual step-by-step apply guide per country. comes out as a spreadsheet + pdfs,
> not a chat log
>
> doing the first few free cause i want examples i can show people. want me to run yours

Short. No adjectives. "Doing the first few free cause I want examples" is the whole pitch — it's
honest, it explains itself, and it makes them a collaborator instead of a customer.

## 2. Opener — friend-of-a-friend / cold-ish

> hey — [name] mentioned you're applying [UK/aus/etc] this year?
>
> i do uni research for people. not advice, research: i pull 20-40 unis that fit your grades +
> budget, verify the fees and deadlines against the official pages, cut it to a shortlist, then write
> you an apply guide per country. you get a spreadsheet (41 columns per uni) + pdfs you keep
>
> RM50. happy to send you a sample of what someone else's looked like first, then you decide

Colder market needs the proof offered up front, and a price stated plainly. Never make a stranger ask
what it costs.

## 3. "Gemini can do this" — the 2-line reply

**This is the one you'll use most. Resist the urge to write more.**

> yeah honestly for a lot of stuff it can
>
> [paste the 41 columns]
>
> this is mine tho. want me to just run yours and you compare

**Why it works:** "yeah honestly it can" is disarming — you agreed, so there's nothing to push
against. Then the artifact does the arguing. You never claimed superiority; they just looked at it.

## 4. "Gemini can do this" — the long reply

Only when they actually want to debate. Pick **one** of these, not all four:

> it can, i use it too. three things it won't do though and it's not a prompt thing:
>
> **1.** ask it for the best unis for you and it'll put ones you can't get into at the top. it can't
> tell "this is a great uni" apart from "you can actually get in" — mine has those as two separate
> columns. did your list tell you which ones were reaches?
>
> **2.** it won't tell you when it's guessing. it'll give you a tuition fee from 2022 like it's fact.
> every row of mine says whether i got it from the official page or an aggregator, and what year the
> number is from. fees are the whole decision — that's a bad number to be quietly wrong about
>
> **3.** it forgets you. applications take like 6 months. next week you're re-typing your grades and
> budget and getting a different answer. mine's written down in files you keep

The killer question is **#1** — *"did your list tell you which ones were reaches?"* It didn't, and
they'll know it didn't. Ask it and let them sit with it.

## 5. "Why so cheap / what's the catch"

Good sign. Means they think it's worth more.

> no catch, i'm just early. i've done the full thing properly for exactly one person so far. i want
> like 5 proper examples i can show people, and you're one of them if you want
>
> so it's free for you. if it's rubbish just tell me and pay nothing, i'd rather know. RM50 once i've
> got the examples

**Never** justify the price with value ("it's worth way more!"). Justify it with your **stage**.
"I'm early, I need proof" is true, it's disarming, and it can't be argued with.

## 6. "How do I know your info is even right"

The best question anyone will ask you. Answer it precisely.

> cause i don't trust it either lol
>
> everything from an aggregator (uni rankings sites, studyportals etc) is only used to *find* unis.
> before anything makes it to your shortlist i re-check the fees, entry reqs and deadlines against the
> actual uni page / ucas / common app. every row literally says whether it's been checked yet or not
>
> and if a uni's page says something different from a rankings site, official wins and i note the
> conflict

Confidence here comes from the *mechanism*, not from insisting you're careful. Anyone can claim care.
Only you can show an `Info source` column that says `Not verified` on the rows you haven't checked yet.

## 7. Parent-facing

**Different pitch entirely.** Parents don't care about features. Parents care about not being wrong
about RM400k. Lead with money, recognition, deadlines. Never lead with AI.

> Hi Aunty/Uncle — I put together the university research for [name].
>
> It's a spreadsheet comparing each option on **total cost in ringgit for the whole degree**, not
> per-year — a 3-year UK degree and a 4-year Australian one look very different once you compare them
> properly. Fees, living costs, and the money you need to show for the visa are all in there.
>
> It also flags which ones are **recognised in Malaysia** [for MMC/BEM/LPQB if relevant], which
> scholarships [name] can realistically apply for and how competitive each one is, and every deadline
> in one list so nothing gets missed.
>
> Everything's checked against the universities' own pages, and each row shows where the number came
> from and what year it's from — so you can check any of it yourself.

Note what's absent: no AI, no "powered by," no adjectives. A parent wants to hear that the numbers are
checkable and comparable. That's it.

## 8. The disqualifier

Use this **honestly** and it will make you more sales than any pitch here.

> honestly? if you already know your course, know you're going uk, and you've already got your 3-4
> unis picked — just use gemini or the ucas site. you don't need me
>
> where i'm actually useful is if you're comparing across countries (working out uk vs aus vs us in
> ringgit properly is the annoying bit), or you're not sure what course, or budget is tight enough
> that it's the deciding thing, or you need it recognised in malaysia for like medicine/engineering/law

Saying "you don't need me" to someone who doesn't is what makes it mean something when you tell the
next person they do. And they'll refer you the friend who *does* need it — nobody refers a salesman.

## 9. "Can't my school counsellor do this"

> yeah and you should definitely still talk to them — they know you and i don't
>
> counsellors just don't have time to do 30 unis per student properly. mine's more the grunt work:
> here's 40 options costed out in ringgit, here's which are reaches vs safeties, here's the deadlines.
> bring it to your counsellor, honestly. it's better input for that conversation than starting from
> nothing

Never position against a human who knows the student. Position as **input to** them. You lose nothing
and you sound like someone with perspective instead of someone selling.

## 10. Follow-up (they went quiet)

Once. Never twice.

> no worries if you're not doing this yet — deadlines aren't till [month] anyway
>
> if you want it later just tell me, takes me a couple days. good luck with [exams/whatever]

Then drop it. At RM50 chasing is worth less than your reputation among 18-year-olds who all talk to
each other.

## Things to never say

| Don't | Why |
|---|---|
| "Gemini can't do this" | Tells them they've been using their tool wrong. They'll defend it, not consider you. |
| "AI-powered / comprehensive solution" | Instantly reads as a scam. You're a guy with a spreadsheet who did the work. That's stronger. |
| "It's worth way more than RM50" | Justifies price with value. Justify with your stage instead: "I'm early, I need examples." |
| "I've helped loads of students" | You've completed one. Somebody will ask. "One" is fine as a confession, fatal as a caught lie. |
| "This uni is perfect for you" | The dossiers include *reasons to hesitate*. Say that instead — it's the most credible thing you have. |
