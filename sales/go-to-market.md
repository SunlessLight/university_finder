# Go-to-market — the advice memo

Short version of the whole thing: **your problem is that people can't *see* the product, and you fix
that with one asset, not with advertising.** Everything below expands that.

Companion files: [service-tiers.md](service-tiers.md) (what's in the package),
[positioning.md](positioning.md) (why the Gemini framing works), [scripts.md](scripts.md) (the
copy-paste messages).

---

## 1. The diagnosis: this is a visibility problem, not a persuasion problem

The student thinks they're buying a spreadsheet, so at RM50 they think *"Gemini can make me a
spreadsheet."* They're not wrong about that — and arguing with them just tells them they've been using
their tool wrong. The real gap is that **they've never seen the dossiers or the apply guide**, so they
can't value them. You can't out-argue an invisible product. You can only show it.

Your own [positioning.md §3](positioning.md) already says this is the single highest-converting move:
*show, don't debate.* The problem is that until now there's been nothing to show. That's the one thing
worth fixing first.

## 2. The service, sectioned: a 3-tier ladder

Full detail in [service-tiers.md](service-tiers.md). In one screen:

| Tier | Name | What they get | The line |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **The List** | 20–40 → verified **8–12** unis · 34-col spreadsheet · Reach/Match/Safety · total cost in MYR | *Know your real options, costed in ringgit* |
| **2** | **The Dossiers** | 3–5 deep **16-section dossier PDFs** · "who actually gets in" · reasons to hesitate | *Can I get in, will I belong, what will it take* |
| **3** | **The Apply Pack** | recommendation · one **deadline calendar** · per-region **apply-guide PDFs** grouped by system | *Now go do it — nothing missed* |

Why tier it rather than sell one blob: (a) it makes the invisible part (Tiers 2–3) **nameable**, which
is the whole point; (b) it gives you a natural upsell later — the free 5 get everything, but once you
charge you can price "just the list" vs "the full thing"; (c) it matches how the pipeline actually
works (depth escalates as the list narrows).

## 3. Should you make a sample PDF? **Yes — it's the priority, above everything else here.**

Not a brochure of features — a **real, redacted output the student can flip through.** The
[sample pack](sample_pack/) is exactly this: one row of the spreadsheet, one full dossier PDF, one
apply-guide page. Redacted from the one student who's been through the whole pipeline end-to-end.

Why the sample beats a designed pitch document: a brochure makes *claims*, which is the exact thing
they don't believe. A real dossier PDF makes them go *"oh — Gemini didn't give me any of this."* They
sell themselves and you didn't spend a word. The one-page pitch ([pitch/](pitch/)) still exists — but
its job is just the cover/menu that *points at* the sample. The sample does the selling.

**Redaction is a hard gate.** The sample is built from a real student's data (PII). It gets scrubbed,
then **you eyeball it before it's shared or committed** — never auto-published. See the sample pack's
own README for what was stripped.

## 4. Should you advertise online / Facebook / socials? **Not paid ads — not yet, probably not for a long time.**

This is the one place I'd stop you. Paid ads are the wrong tool for where you are:

- **You're at n = 1.** Ads amplify. You have nothing to amplify yet and no social proof to convert a
  cold stranger. You'd be paying to send people to a thing that can't yet prove itself.
- **Paid ads fight your own strategy.** Your entire edge is *"if it reads like marketing copy, it reads
  like a scam"* — RM50, friends-first, trust-based, honest-disarm. A Facebook ad is marketing copy to
  strangers. It undoes the exact thing that makes you credible.
- **The economics don't work.** At RM50 a sale, paid acquisition will often cost more per click than
  you make per customer. You'd lose money to look busy.
- **Your real distribution channel is the artifact itself.** 18-year-olds applying to uni all talk to
  each other. A forwarded sample pack is worth more than any ad, because it arrives from a friend, not
  from you. **Make the demo the ad.**

What you *should* do instead, in order:

1. **Finish the 5 free.** You can't sell a track record you don't have. (positioning.md §5.)
2. **Turn each into an anonymized case study** — a screenshot, a testimonial, permission to show it.
3. **Assemble/keep the one shareable sample pack** and drop it into DMs using the existing
   [scripts.md](scripts.md).
4. **Organic only, and only now:** *your own* IG story / a post showing a redacted case study —
   "here's what I built for a friend." Organic is fine because it's you being a person, not you buying
   reach. This is where socials belong, *after* step 1.
5. **Paid ads: only if** warm + referral + organic genuinely tap out and you have a proven conversion
   rate to spend against. That's a much later problem, if ever.

## 5. The sequence, one line

**Finish the 5 free → case study each → one shareable sample pack → DMs + organic case-study posts →
(much later, maybe) paid ads.** Proof first. Show, don't advertise.

## 6. What NOT to change

Your [positioning.md](positioning.md) and [scripts.md](scripts.md) are already good — better than most.
Don't polish them into marketing. The voice rules hold across every new asset too: **no "AI-powered /
comprehensive solution," numbers over adjectives, and always keep the "reasons to hesitate" honesty** —
it's the most credible thing you have.
